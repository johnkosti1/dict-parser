fuss

From Longman Dictionary of Contemporary Englishfussfuss1 /fʌs/ ●○○ noun [singular, uncountable]    1 PROBLEManxious behaviour or activity that is usually about unimportant things  James said he’d better be getting back or there’d be a fuss.  The Steamatic enables you to clean any carpet with the minimum of fuss.2 ATTENTIONattention or excitement that is usually unnecessary or unwelcome  They wanted a quiet wedding without any fuss.  Until I heard her sing I couldn’t see what all the fuss was about (=why people liked it so much).3 → make a fuss/kick up a fuss (about something)4 → make a
