You say: I heard a strange sound. ✗Don’t say: I was hearing a strange sound.• Hear is often used with can: I could hear a strange sound.• In spoken English, people sometimes say I’m hearing to talk about something they have been told recently, especially more than once: I’ve been hearing some nice things about you.USAGE: Hear or listen?Don’t confuse hear (=a sound comes into your ears) with listen to (=hear and pay attention to something).You say: You should listen to my advice. ✗Don’t say: You should hear my advice.You say: I lay in bed listening to music. ✗Don’t say: I lay in bed hearing music.THESAURUShear to know that a sound is being made, using your earsThere’s no need to shout – I can hear you!Voices could be heard in the distance.listen to pay attention to something, using your earsI was listening to the news on the car radio.
