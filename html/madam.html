From Longman Dictionary of Contemporary Englishmadammad‧am /ˈmædəm/ ●○○ noun    1 TALK TO somebodyWOMAN formal used to address a woman in a polite way, especially a customer in a shop SYN ma'am American English  Are you being served, Madam?2 → Dear Madam3 → Madam President/Ambassador etc4 [countable]SY a woman who is in charge of a brothel5 → a (proper) little madamExamples from the Corpusmadam• How do you do, Madam?• But, anyway, I said yes, because I thought I could escape from him easier than from madam.• She wanted to be that kind of madam.• Or do you prefer whisky-and-soda, madam?• As the madam, Daly received the £10 door money.• She was madam, all right, a proper little madam.
