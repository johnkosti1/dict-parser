talent

From Longman Dictionary of Contemporary Englishtalenttal‧ent /ˈtælənt/ ●●● W3 noun    1 [countable, uncountable]GOOD AT a natural ability to do something well  He has a lot of talent, and his work is fresh and interesting.talent for  She showed a talent for acting at an early age.  a persuasive speaker with a natural talent for leadership  His latest book reveals hidden talents.  Sadly, she inherited none of her father’s musical talent.  Your brother is a man of many talents.  There’s a wealth of talent in English football.► see thesaurus at skill 2 [uncountable]GOOD AT a person or
