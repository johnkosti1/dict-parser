ignore

From Longman Dictionary of Contemporary Englishignoreig‧nore /ɪɡˈnɔː $ -ˈnɔːr/ ●●● S2 W2 AWL verb [transitive]    1 IGNOREto deliberately pay no attention to something that you have been told or that you know about  You can’t ignore the fact that many criminals never go to prison.  problems which we can’t afford to ignore2 IGNOREto behave as if you had not heard or seen someone or something  The phone rang, but she ignored it.  Sam rudely ignored the question.completely/totally ignore somebody/something  He had completely ignored her remark, preferring his own theory.RegisterIn everyday
