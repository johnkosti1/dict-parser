huge

From Longman Dictionary of Contemporary Englishhugehuge /hjuːdʒ/ ●●● S1 W2 adjective    1 BIGextremely large in size, amount, or degree SYN enormous  a huge dog  huge crowds  Your room’s huge compared to mine.  These shoes make my feet look huge.a huge amount/sum/quantity etc  huge sums of money  We are only now realizing the huge scale of the problem.a huge loss/profit/increase etc  a huge increase in costa huge range/variety/selection etc  a huge range of issuesa huge success/disappointment etc  The play was a huge success.a huge difference/gap etc  The new system has made a huge difference.► see thesaurus at big 2 informal very popular or famous  David Hasselhoff is huge in Germany. —hugeness noun [uncountable]GrammarHuge is not used with ‘very’.
