From Longman Dictionary of Contemporary Englishdiscoverdis‧cov‧er /dɪsˈkʌvə $ -ər/ ●●● S2 W1 verb [transitive]    1 to find someone or something, either by accident or because you were looking for them  The body was discovered in a field.  Forest Service crews often discover campfires that have not been put out completely.► see thesaurus at find2 FIND OUTto find out something that you did not know about before  The exercises let students discover math concepts on their own.discover (that)  She discovered that she was pregnant.discover who/what/how etc  His friends were shocked to discover how ill he was.GrammarIn more formal English, you say it is discovered that something is true: It was discovered that lightning was electrical. 3 FINDif someone discovers a new place, fact, substance etc, they are the first person to find it or know that it exists  The Curies are best known for discovering radium.4 to notice or try something for the first time and start to enjoy it  At fourteen, Louise discovered boys. 5 SUCCESSFULto notice someone who is very good at something and help them to become successful and well known  a band that’s waiting to be discovered —discoverer noun
