GRAMMAR: Countable or uncountable?• Trouble is usually used as an uncountable noun: Are you having trouble with your car? ✗Don’t say: Are you having a trouble with your car? Trouble is not used with ‘a’.• The plural form troubles is used when talking about worries or bad experiences that someone has: Tell me about all your troubles.
