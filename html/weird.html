weird

From Longman Dictionary of Contemporary Englishweirdweird1 /wɪəd $ wɪrd/ ●●● S2 adjective    informalSTRANGE very strange and unusual, and difficult to understand or explain  A really weird thing happened last night.  He’s a weird bloke.  They sell all sorts of weird and wonderful (=very strange) products.► see thesaurus at strange —weirdly adverb  a weirdly shaped rock —weirdness noun [uncountable]Examples from the Corpusweird• I don't really want to spend the evening with Helen -
