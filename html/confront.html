confront

From Longman Dictionary of Contemporary Englishconfrontcon‧front /kənˈfrʌnt/ ●○○ verb [transitive]    1 PROBLEMif a problem, difficulty etc confronts you, it appears and needs to be dealt with  The problems confronting the new government were enormous.be confronted with something  Customers are confronted with a bewildering amount of choice.2 DEAL WITHto deal with something very difficult or unpleasant in a brave and determined way  We try to help people confront their problems.3 THREATENto face someone in a threatening way, as though you are going to attack them  Troops were confronted by an angry mob. 4 ACCUSEto accuse someone of doing something, especially by showing them the proofconfront somebody with/about something  I confronted him with my suspicions, and he admitted everything.  I haven’t confronted her about it yet.→ See Verb tableExamples from the Corpusconfront• At the same time, serious threats to the stability of the infant democracy also had to be
