cope

From Longman Dictionary of Contemporary Englishcopecope1 /kəʊp $ koʊp/ ●●○ S3 W3 verb [intransitive]    1 DEAL WITHto succeed in dealing with a difficult problem or situation  Sometimes I find it hard to cope.  He coped quite well as manager while still captaining the team.cope with  She feared she wouldn’t be able to cope with two new babies.  Local authorities have to cope with the problems of homelessness.2 DOif a system or machine copes with a particular type or amount of work, it does itcope with  No system is designed to cope with the floods we have had this year.  My computer can cope with huge amounts of data.
