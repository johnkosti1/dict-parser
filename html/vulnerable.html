vulnerable

From Longman Dictionary of Contemporary Englishvulnerablevul‧ne‧ra‧ble /ˈvʌlnərəbəl/ ●●○ W3 adjective    1 WEAKsomeone who is vulnerable can be easily harmed or hurt OPP invulnerable  He took advantage of me when I was at my most vulnerable.  We work mainly with the elderly and other vulnerable groups.be vulnerable to something  Children are most vulnerable to abuse within their own home.2
