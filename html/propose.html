everyday English, you propose that someone does something: I propose that Ms Hallam is appointed.• In more formal English, you propose that someone do something, using the base form of the verb (=infinitive without ‘to’): I propose that Ms Hallam be appointed. ✗Don’t say: I propose Ms Hallam to be appointed.• You can also use the base form when talking about the past: I proposed that Ms
