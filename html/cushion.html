From Longman Dictionary of Contemporary EnglishRelated topics: Household, Other gamescushioncush‧ion1 /ˈkʊʃən/ ●●● S3 noun [countable]    1 DHa cloth bag filled with soft material that you put on a chair or the floor to make it more comfortable → pillow  a velvet cushion  a cushion cover2 PROTECTsomething that stops one thing from hitting another thing  Good sports shoes should provide a cushion when running.3 PROTECT[usually singular] something, especially money, that prevents you from being immediately affected by a bad situationcushion against  Savings can act as a cushion against unemployment. 4 DGSthe soft rubber edge of the table used for playing billiards or snookerExamples from the Corpuscushion• In the familiar blue nightgown and flannel robe, Aunt Mary, propped against a
